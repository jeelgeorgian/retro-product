History

1950
The first telephone pager system was patented in 1949 by Alfred J. Gross.One of the first practical paging services was launched in 1950 for physicians in the New York City area.
1960
In 1960, John Francis Mitchell combined elements of Motorola's walkie-talkie and automobile radio technologies to create the first transistorized pager
1962
In 1962, the Bell System, the U.S. telephone monopoly, presented its Bellboy radio paging system at the Seattle World's Fair.
1980
In the mid-1980s, tone and voice radio paging became popular among emergency responders and professionals.
